# User Journeys

**Summary**: The second artifact in the [[question-chain]], covering Q4–Q6: who the internal users are, what they're trying to accomplish, and the step-by-step experience of using the product.

**Sources**: raw/question-chain-v2.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-18

---

**Owner:** PM-B with EM

## Q4: Who are our internal users?

The people inside the organization who use the product to deliver value to customers (from Q3, [[product-brief]]). Roles, not individual names — e.g. settlement analyst, operations manager, client support rep.

Answer format: a list of internal role titles.

## Q5: For each role, what are they trying to accomplish?

One sentence per role: "[Role] needs to _____." Covers both customer roles (Q3) and internal roles (Q4). Stay at the goal level, not the step-by-step level.

Answer format: a list of role + goal pairs.

## Q6: For each role, what is the step-by-step experience?

Walk through the journey for each role: "First they _____, then they _____, ..." Include moments of waiting, decision-making, handoffs, and leaving the product. Note where different roles' journeys overlap or connect.

Answer format: a step-by-step journey per role, in plain language.

These journeys feed directly into [[capabilities-and-rules]] — every capability in Q7 must trace back to a step here.
